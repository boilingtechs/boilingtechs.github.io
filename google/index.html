An overview of Google Ads.

WHAT ARE GOOGLE ADS?

Google Ads is, without a doubt, the largest paid ads platform in the world. The goal of Google Ads is to help businesses, both big and small, to target their marketing and boost their sales and conversions. This is done by putting your ads at the top of organic search results for keywords that are related to your business, product, or service. This means that when someone searches for a keyword that you want to target, you can place an ad at the top of the rankings, improving your exposure and increasing the likelihood of a sale.

Google Ads are done on a pay-per-click model. This means that you can post your ads for free, but you have to pay Google each time someone clicks one of your ads. This is done through a bidding process where the highest bidder is the one that gets the exposure on the search results. It is a bit of a complicated process, especially at first, so many businesses choose to partner with an ads agency that has experience getting killer results with Google Ads services.

WHAT ARE THE TYPES OF GOOGLE ADS?

While Google Search ads are the most popular type of ad, there are various ad types that Google offers. It’s important to know the differences of each type of ad as they are served on different platforms and have their own unique set of pros and cons. Aside from Search Ads, Display Ads are quite common. These are the ads that show up across the millions of partner websites and apps – many times, these ads have images and other creative elements.

Google also offers Video Ads. These types of ads will primarily be shown on YouTube, which is also owned by Google. Video ads are (obviously) in video format and can be shown before, during, or after whatever YouTube videos you wish to target. Google Shopping Ads are also quite popular for eCommerce companies and show up in the search results as well. Finally, Google also offers Retargeting Ads where you can remarket your products on partner websites to people who have already visited your website.

HOW DOES GOOGLE AD BIDDING WORK?

All of Google’s Ads are done via a bidding process. Google has an enormous amount of ad placement possibilities across the web, so whenever one of those spaces are available, Google holds an auction and gives the ad placement to the highest bidder. Once you place a bid, you commit to publishing your ad and paying Google anytime someone clicks on that ad.

When making bids for Google Ads, it’s extremely important to have clear goals in mind and ensure that everything is executed correctly. If you set up something incorrectly, you risk spending more money than you have budgeted. This is why we always recommend that your business works with an experienced professional or agency who will be able to set up the campaign in a way that is set up for success.

HOW DO I START ADVERTISING ON GOOGLE?

While executing a high-level ad campaign can take time to learn, setting up your first campaign is fairly simple. First, you’ll want to head to ads.google.com, where you will set up your account with all of the necessary information. Be sure to select ‘Switch to Expert Mode’ – it will give you access to a wider range of features. From there, you will select which ad campaign you are looking to begin. Do you want to do Search Ads? Video Ads? Shopping Ads? Once you have chosen the ad type, you will focus on targeting. Be sure to select a demographic that is in line with your target audience.

Next up, you’ll select your budget, as well as your max bid. This is where you’ll pick very important goals for your campaign, such as conversions, conversion value, impression share, clicks, and more. Once everything is ready, you’ll want to select your ad groups and keywords. Finally, you will want to set up your bidding strategy – you can do an advanced automated strategy or choose to set it manually.

HOW DO I MANAGE MY GOOGLE ADS CAMPAIGN?

While it’s important to set up your ad campaign correctly, this is only the tip of the iceberg. The real challenge will be monitoring the campaign and making the right decisions to optimize your campaign. We recommend regularly checking your metrics – this includes your clicks, impressions, click-through rates, and (most importantly) how much everything is costing you.

To properly manage your campaign, it’s important to look at your Google Ads like a funnel. Start at the top, and look for opportunities to optimize every step of the process. Are impressions low? If so, you might want to reconsider your bidding strategy. Are clicks low? Maybe your copy isn’t magnetic enough. Are your conversions not up to par? Then you might want to consider redesigning your landing page. One of the best ways to optimize your campaign is to run A/B tests.

HOW MUCH DO GOOGLE AD SERVICES COST?

The simple answer is, it varies. You see, the great thing about Google Ads services is there is no universal cost. Google understands that every company has its own niche and unique business model. Therefore the cost per click for an injury law firm will vary drastically from the cost per click from a company that sells leather wallets. The cost of your Google Ads campaign will also vary depending on your budget and your company’s goals.

This is why it’s absolutely crucial to have someone experienced running your ad campaign. Bidding, in particular, can be a complicated process, so if you want to stay within your budget (while still generating a stellar ROI), someone needs to monitor and manage the campaign on a day-to-day basis. A truly great advertising agency will have a detailed approach to auction-time bidding, and they will know how to take advantage of contextual signals.

WHAT IS A LANDING PAGE?

A landing page on your website is designated for one singular purpose – typically to generate sales or leads. Landing pages need to be very detailed, very focused, and designed in a way that optimizes conversions. Regarding Google Ads, it’s essential to have a landing page that is directly related to your advertisement. The more relevant your landing pages are to your Google Search Ad, the higher your likelihood of generating conversions.

For example, let’s say that you own an eCommerce business, and you want to run ads promoting a discount code for a specific product. Once someone clicks on your ad, what do you think will convert better… Sending that user to your general homepage? Or send them to a landing page specific to the product of the ad they clicked on? If you guessed the landing page, then you are correct! The better and more specific your landing pages, the more conversion you can expect to generate.

HOW DO I MEASURE GOOGLE ADS PERFORMANCE?

Measuring your Google Ads performance is easy in some ways and complicated in other ways. It’s easy because Google loves to give you data. There’s tons of it! You can find statistics about your impressions, click-through rate, clicks, bidding, and so much more. But, with so much data available, it can be complicated to know exactly what to look for and (just as importantly) what changes you need to make.

If you are running a Google Ads campaign yourself, we recommend starting by looking at your click-through rate. This metric is the basis of any Google Ads campaign – after all, you can’t make a sale if you aren’t getting clicks! If your click through is not what you were hoping for, consider modifying the keywords you are targeting or running an A/B test with a different copy. Cost-per-conversion is another crucial metric as it will give you a better idea of your customer acquisition costs.
